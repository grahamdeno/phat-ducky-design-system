The Character

Voice & Attitude

Phat Ducky is the mascot who already made it and isn't shy about it — gold fronts, a chain heavy enough to matter, a flat-brim cap cocked just enough. He talks like he runs the block, not like he runs a board meeting.

Confident, not corporate

Short lines. No hedging, no filler words, no "just checking in." He says the thing once, and it lands.

Deadpan grin

The humor is dry and self-aware — cocky enough to be funny, never mean enough to punch down.

Gold is earned, not sprayed

Same rule on the page as around his neck: one gold moment per layout, never gold-on-gold-on-gold.

Street vernacular, kept crisp

Lean on real language ("cop this," "the drop," "fresh") — but every sentence still has to scan clean.

Color System

5 Core + 3 Accent

Two layers. The five client-supplied tones build every surface, panel, and line of body copy — they're muted on purpose so nothing competes with the mark. Three saturated accents, pulled straight off the artwork, are reserved for moments that need to pop: calls to action, badges, drop alerts.

03

Typography

Display / Body / Tag

Three roles, three jobs. A heavyweight display face carries the swagger; a plain grotesk carries the reading; a monospace does the paperwork — hex codes, prices, hang-tag labels — like a stitched-in size tag.

Cop This Drop

Display — Arial Black / 900 / uppercase — hero lines, section titles, price call-outs

The mascot leads every layout, but the copy still has to hold up a paragraph. Body text runs in a plain, honest grotesk with no attitude of its own — it gets out of the way so the gold can do its job.

Clear space on every side equals the height of the gold D badge on the cap. Nothing — text, edge, other logos — sits inside that margin.

Do

Keep the gold gradient intact — highlight to bronze, never a flat fill.
Place on Ink, Umber, or Bone grounds, where the outline holds contrast.
Give it room — honor the clear-space rule above, always.

Don't

Recolor the mascot outside this palette — no brand pink, no neon blue.
Stretch, skew, rotate, or add an outer glow beyond this spec.
Set the wordmark in any face but the approved display weight.
